# Recommendation Summary

## Finding
- Total dataset: 9,994 orders across 2014–2017
- Tables and Bookcases are loss-makers despite significant sales volume:
  - Tables: -$17,725 total profit on $206,965 in sales (-8.56% margin)
  - Bookcases: -$3,472 total profit on $114,880 in sales (-3.02% margin)
- Technology dominates profitability:
  - Copiers: $55,617 profit at 37.2% margin
  - Phones: $44,515 profit at 13.49% margin
  - Accessories: $41,936 profit at 25.05% margin
- Consumer segment generates the highest revenue ($1.16M) but has the 
  lowest profit margin at 11.55%
- Home Office generates the least revenue ($429K) but maintains the 
  highest margin at 14.03%

## Root Cause
- Aggressive discounting is the primary driver of margin erosion:
  - No discount → average profit of $66.90 per order
  - 1–20% discount → average profit drops to $26.50 per order
  - 21–40% discount → average profit turns negative at -$77.86 per order
  - Above 40% discount → average profit reaches -$106.71 per order
- 933 orders (nearly 10% of all transactions) fall in the above 40% band
- Furniture sub-categories  particularly Tables  receive the heaviest 
  discounts, explaining their persistent losses despite strong sales volume

## Recommendation
- **Cap discounts at 20% across all categories** 

  This is the threshold 
  beyond which orders become unprofitable on average
- **Conduct a pricing review for Tables and Bookcases** 
 
  If discounting 
  cannot be reduced, consider discontinuing or repositioning these products
- **Prioritize Technology sub-categories** 

  Particularly Copiers and Paper, 
  which combine high absolute profit with margins above 37%
- **Target the Consumer segment for discount reduction**  Highest volume 
  but lowest margin, meaning small margin improvements have the largest 
  overall impact on profitability
